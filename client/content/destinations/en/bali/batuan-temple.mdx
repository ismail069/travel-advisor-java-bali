## Overview — English

Pura Puseh Desa Batuan, commonly known to visitors as Batuan Temple, is one of the oldest and most beautiful temples in Bali. Founded in 1022 AD (11th century), this temple is a masterpiece of classic Balinese architecture. Visitors are immediately captivated by the highly detailed red brick carvings, black thatched roofs, and the majestic split gate ( *candi bentar* ) at the entrance.

As an active place of worship deeply embedded in the daily lives of the local community, visitors are required to follow strict etiquette. Modest dress is mandatory. You must cover your shoulders and knees, and wear a traditional sarong and sash to enter the temple grounds. If you do not have your own, the use of a sarong is typically included in your entrance fee and provided at the ticket counter.

Strolling through the temple courtyards feels like stepping back in time. Take your time to observe the ancient stone carvings depicting Hindu mythology and scenes from traditional Balinese life.
