## Overview — English

Located just a few kilometers south of central Ubud, Mas Village is widely recognized as the premier wood carving center of Bali. The village has a long history of producing master carvers whose works adorn temples, palaces, and nowadays, art galleries around the globe. Unlike mass-produced souvenir crafts, the wood carvings from Mas Village highlight a distinctive style that blends humanism and naturalism.

There is no entrance fee to visit Mas Village. You are free to stroll along the village streets lined with art galleries and family workshops. The highlight of a visit here is watching the carving process in person. Artisans typically work in open areas within their family compounds and are often happy to demonstrate how they chisel blocks of wood—such as teak, mahogany, or ebony—into incredibly fine and expressive details.

While many tourists visit the large showrooms on tour buses, exploring the smaller, independent workshops tucked away in side alleys is highly recommended. If you find a piece you love, haggling is common, though keep in mind that the price reflects the quality of the wood and the weeks or months of labor invested.
