## Overview — English

Puri Saren Agung, widely known as Ubud Palace, stands in the center of Ubud and remains connected to the local royal family. Visitors typically see the front courtyards, gateways, stone carvings, and traditional buildings open to the public. The entire complex is not accessible because it is more than a tourist attraction.

A daytime visit is relatively short and combines easily with Ubud Art Market, Museum Puri Lukisan, or Saraswati Temple. In the evening, selected courtyards host Balinese dance performances. Program, schedule, capacity, and ticket prices can change and must be checked again.

Current online sources do not fully agree on general access hours. Treat published hours as a planning guide and confirm close to the visit.

## Practical Planning / Perencanaan Praktis

Pusat Ubud padat, terutama menjelang siang dan sore. Walking route dapat mengurangi kebutuhan parkir, tetapi trotoar, penyeberangan, panas, dan hujan tetap perlu diperhitungkan. Untuk museum, periksa apakah tiket mencakup minuman, workshop, pertunjukan, atau akses khusus.
