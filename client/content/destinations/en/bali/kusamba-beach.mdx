## Overview — English

Located in the Klungkung Regency, Kusamba Beach offers a fascinating glimpse into authentic Balinese coastal life, far removed from the island's modern tourist hubs. The beach features sparkling dark volcanic sand lined with fleets of colorful traditional outrigger fishing boats (Jukung). While it serves as a busy port for fast boats heading to Nusa Penida, Kusamba's primary draw for cultural travelers is its **traditional artisanal sea salt production**.

The salt-making process here has been passed down through generations. Farmers use an ancient technique that involves rhythmically splashing seawater onto the volcanic sand to evaporate in the sun. The salt-crusted sand is then gathered and filtered, and the resulting pure brine is left to crystallize in long troughs hollowed out from coconut tree trunks. Kusamba salt is highly prized for its purity and rich mineral flavor. Visitors are welcome to observe this laborious process and are highly encouraged to buy salt directly from the farmers to help preserve this fading heritage craft.
