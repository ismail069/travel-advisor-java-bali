## Overview — English

Uluwatu Temple occupies a dramatic cliff-edge setting at Bali’s southern tip, overlooking the Indian Ocean. Visitors come to explore the temple grounds, walk along the cliff paths, and—when schedules align—watch the Kecak performance near sunset.

The complex remains a sacred site. Sarongs and sashes are generally required under local rules, while some inner areas are restricted to worshippers. Macaques move freely around the grounds, so glasses, hats, phones, bottles, and food should be secured.

The visit works best when you arrive early enough to walk without rushing and then move toward the performance area before capacity fills. Kecak schedules and ticket arrangements should be checked directly because they can change.

### Main Highlights

- Indian Ocean cliff views
- Temple architecture and sacred context
- Sunset atmosphere
- Kecak performance

### Practical Planning

The destination should be planned around current operating conditions, weather, crowd patterns, and cultural rules. Travel time in Bali is variable, so avoid treating map estimates as guaranteed arrival times.
