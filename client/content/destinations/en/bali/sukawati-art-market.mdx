## Overview — English

Sukawati Art Market is one of Bali's oldest and most renowned traditional markets, located on Jalan Raya Sukawati in Gianyar. It is a haven for travelers looking to purchase Balinese souvenirs, including clothing (such as sarongs and resort wear), paintings, wood carvings, silver jewelry, and a myriad of local handicrafts.

Shopping at Sukawati offers a cultural experience in itself, as haggling is expected and part of the daily trading culture. Vendors typically quote a high initial price, so good bargaining skills are essential—it's common to negotiate down to 30-50% of the asking price.

Because the market can get very hot and congested during the day, the best time to visit is early in the morning. Not only is the weather cooler, but local vendors also believe that the first sale of the day brings good luck (a concept called *penglaris*), making them more willing to offer significant discounts to early-bird shoppers.
