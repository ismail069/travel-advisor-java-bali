## Overview — English

Batuan Village is one of the oldest cultural hubs in Bali, renowned for two main attractions: its unique traditional painting style (the Batuan Style) and the 11th-century Pura Puseh Batuan (Batuan Temple).

The Batuan painting style is highly distinctive, characterized by dark colors, incredibly dense details that leave almost no empty space on the canvas, and themes that blend Hindu mythology with scenes of everyday Balinese life. Throughout the village, you can visit local artists' homes and art galleries to watch the painstaking process of creating these paintings, which can take months to finish. Entry to these home studios is generally free, but purchasing a piece directly from the creator is the best way to support this enduring art form.

Beyond the art galleries, a visit to Batuan is incomplete without stopping at Batuan Temple. The temple features stunning, intricately carved ancient brick gates and well-preserved pavilions. You are required to wear a sarong (typically provided at the ticket counter) to enter the sacred temple grounds.
