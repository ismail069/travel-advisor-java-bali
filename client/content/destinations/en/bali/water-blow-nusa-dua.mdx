## Overview — English

Water Blow sits at the edge of Peninsula Nusa Dua within The Nusa Dua district. ITDC describes it as part of the Nusa Dua coast where powerful waves strike the rock formation and create bursts of sea spray.

The effect is not equally strong at all times. Tide, wind direction, wave period, and general sea conditions influence the height of the spray. Visitors may arrive on a day when the display is modest, which does not mean the site is inactive.

The value of the visit lies in observing ocean power from the designated viewing area. This is not a swimming spot, not a place to stand on wet rocks, and not a location where barriers should be crossed for photographs. The best experience depends on patience, safe positioning, and acceptance that nature cannot be scheduled.

## Why Visit?

- Strong natural spectacle when conditions align.
- Easy to combine with Nusa Dua Beach and Peninsula Island.
- Short visit suitable for a half-day South Bali itinerary.
- Good photography potential without requiring a long hike.
- Managed access compared with completely open cliff sites.

## Best Time to Visit

### Morning

Morning usually offers lower heat and more comfortable walking conditions. Wave strength is not guaranteed by time of day alone.

### High Tide and Stronger Swell

The effect may be more dramatic when ocean conditions push more water into the rock channel. Do not use this as a reason to approach exposed areas.

### Rainy and Windy Conditions

Rain and wind can increase spray, reduce footing, and make the area less comfortable. Severe weather should lead to postponement, not risk-taking.

## Ticket Information

The operator has used separate admission categories in the past, but historical tariffs should not be treated as current prices. Confirm the applicable visitor category and price directly before travel.
