## Overview — English

Tulamben is a small fishing village on Bali's northeast coast that has evolved into one of the world's most renowned scuba diving destinations. The shoreline here is entirely devoid of sand; instead, it is composed of large, smooth, black volcanic stones, a legacy of Mount Agung's past eruptions.

The defining attraction of Tulamben is the **USAT Liberty Shipwreck**, an American cargo ship torpedoed by a Japanese submarine during World War II. The ship was originally beached on the shore, but the catastrophic eruption of Mount Agung in 1963 pushed the massive vessel back into the sea. Today, it rests a mere 30 meters from the shoreline at depths ranging from 3 to 30 meters. Because it is so shallow and close to the beach, this spectacular, coral-encrusted artificial reef is accessible even to beginner divers and snorkelers.
