## Overview — English

Kerta Gosa is a surviving pavilion complex of the Klungkung Royal Palace (Puri Semarapura), originally built in the late 17th century. The name "Kerta Gosa" translates roughly to "the place where the king meets with his ministries to discuss justice." Historically, it functioned as the kingdom's supreme court, where the king and Brahman priests presided over legal disputes that could not be resolved at the village level.

The most spectacular feature of Kerta Gosa is its highly intricate, classical **Kamasan-style** ceiling paintings. These two-dimensional murals served as moral education for the defendants awaiting trial below. The panels graphically depict the horrifying punishments of hell (bad karma) and the rewards of heaven (good karma), alongside stories from the Mahabharata epic. The complex also features the **Bale Kambang**, a beautiful floating pavilion surrounded by a lotus pond, historically used for royal religious ceremonies.
