## Overview — English

Neka Art Museum is one of Ubud’s significant art museums. It was established to collect, preserve, document, and present art connected with Bali. Its collections are not limited to one period or one group of artists.

Visitors can see work by Balinese, Indonesian, Asian, and Western artists inspired by Bali. Multiple pavilions help separate themes, periods, and collection types. The official museum material also describes sculpture, photography, keris, Affandi, and Arie Smit sections.

The museum is most rewarding for visitors willing to read labels and follow the development of the collections. It is not a one-photo stop, but a place to understand how Balinese art moved from classical styles toward modern expression.

## Koleksi dan Paviliun

### Balinese Painting

Koleksi memperlihatkan perkembangan lukisan Bali dan hubungan antara seniman lokal, patron, kolektor, serta seniman asing.

### Arie Smit

House of Arie Smit dan koleksi terkait memberi konteks terhadap seniman Belanda yang sangat terkait dengan perkembangan kelompok Young Artists di Ubud.

### Affandi Pavilion

Paviliun ini menampilkan karya Affandi dan membantu memperluas konteks museum di luar seni Bali lokal.

### Photography Pavilion

Situs resmi menyebut koleksi foto hitam-putih Robert Koke yang mendokumentasikan seni serta budaya Bali dari tahun 1930-an hingga awal 1940-an.

### Keris Pavilion

Koleksi keris merupakan salah satu bagian khas museum dan membantu menjelaskan keris sebagai benda seni, budaya, dan spiritual.

### Sculpture Pavilion

Patung klasik, primitif, dan modern memberi gambaran mengenai beragam perkembangan seni patung yang terkait dengan Bali.

## Collections and Pavilions

### Balinese Painting

The collection presents the development of Balinese painting and the relationships between local artists, patrons, collectors, and foreign artists.

### Arie Smit

The House of Arie Smit and related collection provide context for the Dutch artist strongly associated with Ubud’s Young Artists movement.

### Affandi Pavilion

This pavilion presents work by Affandi and broadens the museum’s scope beyond local Balinese art.

### Photography Pavilion

The official museum information describes black-and-white photographs by Robert Koke documenting Balinese art and culture from the 1930s to the early 1940s.

### Keris Pavilion

The keris collection is a distinctive museum section, presenting the dagger as an artistic, cultural, and spiritual object.

### Sculpture Pavilion

Classical, primitive, and modern sculpture illustrates varied developments in art connected with Bali.

## Best Time to Visit

Morning provides cooler conditions and a quieter atmosphere. Early afternoon is also practical, particularly during rain or strong midday heat elsewhere in Ubud.

The museum closes at 17:00 and accepts final entry 30 minutes before closing. Arriving after 16:00 does not provide enough time for a complete visit.

## Ticket Information

The official visitor page states:

- Tickets are purchased in person.
- Tickets include the permanent collection.
- Temporary exhibitions do not require a ticket.

The current tariff is not clearly displayed in the retrieved official text. Confirm admission at the museum before publication.
