## Ikhtisar — Bahasa Indonesia

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park, atau GWK, adalah taman budaya berskala besar di Ungasan. Daya tarik utamanya adalah Patung Garuda Wisnu Kencana, tetapi pengalaman di dalam kawasan juga mencakup plaza batu kapur, patung Wisnu dan Garuda, area pertunjukan, restoran, toko, ruang acara, serta tur tertentu ke bagian dalam patung.

Situs resmi GWK menampilkan peta kawasan dengan banyak titik, termasuk Amphitheater, Plaza Wisnu, Plaza Garuda, Lotus Pond, Festival Park, GWK Statue, Beranda Restaurant, dan Jendela Bali. Skala ini menjelaskan mengapa kunjungan kurang dari satu jam sering terasa terburu-buru.

GWK paling menarik bila pengunjung tidak hanya datang untuk mengambil satu foto. Pertunjukan budaya harian memberi konteks tambahan, sementara waktu menjelang sore dapat mengurangi panas dan memungkinkan wisatawan menunggu program malam.

## Overview — English

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park, commonly called GWK, is a large cultural park in Ungasan. Its central landmark is the Garuda Wisnu Kencana statue, but the wider experience also includes limestone plazas, separate Wisnu and Garuda sculptures, performance venues, restaurants, shops, event spaces, and selected tours inside the statue.

The official GWK website maps numerous locations, including the Amphitheater, Plaza Wisnu, Plaza Garuda, Lotus Pond, Festival Park, the GWK Statue, Beranda Restaurant, and Jendela Bali. This scale explains why a visit of less than one hour often feels rushed.

GWK is most rewarding when visitors do more than take one landmark photograph. Daily cultural programming adds context, while late afternoon reduces heat and allows travelers to remain for evening performances.

## Main Areas

### GWK Statue

The monumental centerpiece depicting Lord Wisnu riding Garuda. Access to special interior or upper-level experiences may require a separate package.

### Plaza Wisnu

An elevated plaza featuring the Wisnu statue and broad views.

### Plaza Garuda

A major open plaza around the Garuda sculpture.

### Lotus Pond

A large event space framed by limestone walls.

### Amphitheater

One of the principal venues for scheduled cultural performances.

### Festival Park and Tlatar Mandalaloka

Large outdoor areas used for events and visitor circulation.

GWK offers selected evening programming and special events. Confirm tickets and return transport in advance.

## Getting There

GWK is located on Jalan Raya Uluwatu in Ungasan.

### From Ngurah Rai Airport

The park is geographically close to the airport, but traffic through Jimbaran and the Bukit Peninsula can be heavy.

### From Nusa Dua

GWK combines well with Nusa Dua Beach or Water Blow. Travel time depends on traffic around South Kuta.

### From Jimbaran

The park is a practical afternoon stop before or after a Jimbaran beach visit.

### Taxi and Ride-Hailing

Services generally operate in the area. During concerts and major events, designated pick-up zones and congestion may affect collection.

## Safety and Comfort

- Use sun protection.
- Carry water.
- Wear comfortable walking shoes.
- Check weather before outdoor performances.
- Follow crowd-control rules during events.
- Do not climb statues or limestone walls.
- Confirm any interior statue-tour restrictions.
- Plan return transport before evening shows.
- Protect children from heat and crowd separation.

## Photography Guide

### Main Statue

Use wide-angle compositions from designated plazas. Leave enough space around the monument to communicate its scale.

### Limestone Walls

The carved limestone corridors and Lotus Pond provide strong geometric backgrounds.

### Performances

Respect photography rules. Avoid flash when it distracts performers.

### Sunset

Higher park areas can provide panoramic late-day light over South Bali.

### Drone

GWK hosts major events and lies within managed airspace. Drone use requires current permission and regulatory compliance.

2. Arriving without checking the performance schedule.
3. Underestimating walking distance.
4. Visiting at midday without sun protection.

6. Ignoring event-day traffic.
7. Not planning return transport after night shows.
8. Using third-party ticket prices as permanent official rates.
