## English

Amed is a coastal area in East Bali known for fishing villages, dark-sand beaches, snorkeling, diving, Mount Agung views, and a quieter atmosphere than Bali's main southern resort areas.

## Safety
- Check currents, waves, and visibility before entering the water.
- Use a dive or snorkeling operator with suitable safety procedures.
- Wear reef-safe sun protection.
- Avoid touching coral or marine animals.
- Use caution on narrow and winding coastal roads.
- Keep valuables secure at beach access points.

## Accessibility
- Accessibility varies by beach, hotel, and activity provider.
- Many shoreline entries include stones, sand, steps, or uneven paths.
- Verify accessible rooms, vehicle access, and assistance directly with providers.

## FAQ

### Is Amed one village?
Amed commonly refers to a wider coastal area containing several villages and beach sections.

### Is snorkeling possible from shore?
Yes at selected locations, but entry conditions and currents vary.

### How long should visitors stay?
One night is possible, while two or three days allows more time for marine activities and nearby excursions.

### Is Amed suitable for beginner divers?
Several local operators serve beginners, but certification, medical, and safety requirements must be confirmed directly.
