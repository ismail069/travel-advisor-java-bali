## Bahasa Indonesia

Gunung Agung adalah gunung tertinggi di Bali dan memiliki makna spiritual yang sangat penting bagi masyarakat Hindu Bali. Pendakian membutuhkan kondisi fisik yang baik, persiapan matang, pemandu yang sesuai, serta pemeriksaan cuaca dan status aktivitas gunung sebelum berangkat.

## English

Mount Agung is Bali's highest mountain and holds major spiritual significance for Balinese Hindus. Climbing requires strong fitness, careful preparation, an appropriate guide, and checks of weather and volcanic activity before departure.

## Highlights
- Sunrise trekking
- High-altitude volcanic landscape
- Panoramic views across Bali
- Sacred cultural significance
- Challenging mountain routes
- Views toward neighboring islands in clear weather

## Safety
- Check official volcanic activity and access advisories.
- Use an experienced local guide.
- Confirm the selected route, difficulty, and estimated duration.
- Carry sufficient water, food, warm layers, rain protection, and a headlamp.
- Wear proper trekking footwear.
- Do not climb during severe weather or restricted access.
- Inform accommodation or a trusted contact of the trekking plan.
- Follow guide instructions and turn back when conditions become unsafe.

## Accessibility
- Summit routes are steep, uneven, and physically demanding.
- The trek is unsuitable for wheelchairs and many visitors with limited mobility.
- Non-hikers can experience views of Mount Agung from surrounding villages and viewpoints.

## Cultural Etiquette
- Respect the mountain's sacred status.
- Follow local restrictions related to ceremonies and religious observances.
- Do not enter restricted temple areas.
- Dress respectfully near temples and villages.
- Carry out all rubbish.
- Avoid disruptive behavior and loud music.

## Suggested Visit Flow
1. Verify volcanic status, weather, route access, and local restrictions.
2. Book a suitable local guide.
3. Prepare equipment, food, water, and transport.
4. Begin at the approved trailhead.
5. Follow the guide's pace and safety decisions.
6. Return through the designated route.
7. Allow recovery time before driving or continuing the itinerary.

## FAQ

### Is a guide required?
Requirements may vary by route and current local rules. Using an experienced local guide is strongly recommended.

### Is Mount Agung suitable for beginners?
The climb is generally demanding. Beginners should discuss fitness, route choice, and conditions with a qualified guide.

### Can access close unexpectedly?
Yes. Weather, volcanic activity, ceremonies, and local safety decisions can affect access.

### Is sunrise guaranteed?
No. Clouds, rain, haze, and seasonal conditions can limit visibility.
